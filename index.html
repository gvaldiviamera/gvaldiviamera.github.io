Short bio

I am a theoretical physicist working on black hole physics, quantum field theory, condensed matter systems, and holography, with a focus on their connections to string theory, quantum information, and quantum many-body dynamics.

My recent work has focused on causal diamond thermodynamics, the Unruh effect and thermofield double states, and near-horizon perspectives on entropy and radiation. I am also interested in strongly correlated quantum matter and SYK-like models as laboratories for holographic duality.

Research interests

  • Black hole physics and quantum aspects of gravity.
  • Quantum field theory and many-body systems.
  • Condensed matter theory and quantum phases of matter.
  • Holography, gauge/gravity duality, and causal diamonds.
  • Connections to string theory and quantum information.
  • SYK-type models, quantum chaos, and holographic quantum matter.

Current and recent topics

  • Causal diamond thermodynamics and horizon entropy.
  • Unruh effect, acceleration radiation, and thermofield double states.
  • Near-horizon perspectives and effective descriptions of radiation.
  • Quantum information aspects of black holes and quantum fields in curved spacetimes.
